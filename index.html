What is a Dunk Calculator?

A Dunk Calculator is an online tool that helps basketball players determine their vertical leap and dunking potential. By entering a few key measurements, you can instantly see if you have the necessary jump height to dunk a basketball.


How Does the Dunking Calculator Work?

Our Vertical Calculator takes into account your:

  • Standing Reach The height you can reach while standing flat-footed.
  • Vertical Jump How high you can jump from a standing or running position.
  • Hoop Height The height of the basketball hoop (standard is 10 feet or 305 cm).
  • Palm Size Your hand’s ability to grip the ball for a dunk.

Once you enter these details, our Dunk Height Calculator will calculate the minimum vertical jump required to dunk and determine whether you can make it or need to improve your jump height.


Vertical Jump Calculator – Measure Your Jump

Our Vertical Jump Calculator helps you find out how high you can jump. To use

  • 1. Stand next to a wall and reach as high as possible (record this height as your Standing Reach).
  • 2. Jump as high as possible and touch the highest point on the wall (record this height).
  • 3. Subtract your Standing Reach from your Jump Height to get your Vertical Leap.

How Much Vertical is Needed to Dunk?

The question "How much to dunk?" depends on your height, standing reach, and ability to jump. Here’s a quick reference guide:

  • If you are 6'0" (183 cm) tall, you need a 28-30 inch vertical jump.
  • If you are 5'10" (178 cm) tall, you need a 32+ inch vertical jump.
  • If you are 5'8" (173 cm) tall, you need at least 36 inches of vertical leap.
  • If you are under 5'6" (167 cm), you will need a 40+ inch vertical to dunk.

Our Dunk Vertical Calculator will give you an exact answer based on your personal stats.


Standing Reach Calculator – Essential for Dunking

Your Standing Reach is the foundation of dunking. Taller players have an advantage, but shorter players with a strong vertical jump can also dunk.


How to Measure Your Standing Reach:

  • Stand against a wall and extend your arm as high as possible.
  • Mark the highest point your fingers reach.
  • Measure from the floor to that mark.

Using our Standing Reach Calculator, you can determine how much additional vertical jump is needed to reach the hoop.


Improve Your Vertical Jump for Dunking

If you’re close but not quite dunking yet, don’t worry! Here are some tips to increase your vertical leap:

  • Strength Training – Focus on squats, deadlifts, and explosive jumps.
  • Plyometrics – Box jumps, depth jumps, and jump rope exercises help improve jumping power.
  • Flexibility & Mobility – Stretching and mobility exercises improve jumping efficiency.
  • Jump Technique – Proper form and momentum can add inches to your vertical.

FAQs About Dunking and Vertical Jump

How can I increase my vertical jump fast?

Train with plyometrics, weightlifting, and flexibility exercises consistently.

What is the average vertical jump for an NBA player?

NBA players average between 28-34 inches, with elite dunkers reaching 40+ inches.

Can I dunk if I’m 5’7”?

Yes, but you’ll need a 40-inch vertical jump to reach the rim.

Is dunking only about vertical leap?

No, hand size, technique, and timing also play a big role.
